## WHEN TO ROLL DICE

**SHOW ROLLS FOR:**
- Combat actions (attacks, defenses, special abilities)
- Dangerous/risky attempts (disarming deadly traps, dangerous climbing, escaping collapse)
- Hostile social interactions (intimidating enemies, deceiving guards, convincing hostile NPCs)
- High-stakes challenges (complex magic rituals, legendary discoveries, life-or-death situations)
- Initial contact with new NPCs (first impression determines their attitude)

**NEVER ROLL FOR:**
- Friendly conversations once rapport is established
- Accepting a quest, agreeing to help, or making a narrative choice
- Player decisions that advance the story without resistance
- Gathering information from cooperative NPCs
- Simple movement or travel decisions
- Routine actions the character is skilled at
- Expressing gratitude, asking polite questions, or basic social courtesy
- Choosing between presented options (paths, equipment, etc.)
- Actions where failure wouldn't create meaningful story consequences

**Key Rule: Only roll when both success AND failure would create interesting story outcomes**

### One Roll Per Interaction
Once you establish an NPC's attitude toward the player (friendly, neutral, hostile), don't keep rolling for the same conversation unless:
- The player attempts something dramatically different
- They're asking for sensitive/dangerous information
- The situation fundamentally changes

**Example:** 
✅ Roll when first approaching the suspicious tracker
❌ Don't roll for each follow-up question once he's being helpful

### Auto-Success Conditions
Characters automatically succeed at tasks well below their skill level:
- **Cooperative NPCs:** No rolls needed for gathering basic information
- **Character expertise:** Auto-succeed on routine tasks within your profession/skills
- **Simple actions:** Basic climbing, obvious perception, polite conversation
- **Low-stakes situations:** Where failure wouldn't meaningfully impact the story

**Combat is NEVER auto-success** - all attacks, defenses, and combat actions require rolls.

No roll needed - simply narrate success and continue story.

## FAILURE CONSEQUENCES

**Critical Rule: Failures create logical consequences that advance the story**

### Consequence Types by Action:
- **Combat:** Miss = counterattack opportunity; Failed defense = take damage/lose position
- **Stealth:** Get spotted by guards, trigger alarms, or alert enemies  
- **Climbing:** Fall and take damage, lose equipment, or alert enemies below
- **Social:** Target becomes hostile, calls for help, or refuses cooperation
- **Lockpicking:** Lock jams, picks break, or trap activates
- **Magic:** Waste mana, spell backfires, or attract unwanted attention

### Narrative Structure:
1. Describe the failed attempt
2. State the immediate consequence  
3. Present new situation with choices

### Example:
You swing at the orc, but it sidesteps your blade. Off-balance, you're exposed as it raises its axe for a counterattack aimed at your ribs.
"Duck and roll!" Emberlyn shouts.

### Rules:
- **Never "try again"** - failure must change the situation
- **Logical consequences** - failed stealth gets you spotted, not struck by lightning  
- **Advance story** - create new problems or complications
- **Maintain momentum** - failures should feel dangerous, not frustrating
